# 2026 IELTS Writing Task 2 Topic Analysis

Source files: 2026 January-May Task 2 model essay collections.

## Executive Summary

Across the 14 questions, the strongest recurring clusters are:

1. Education and youth development
2. Transport, cities, and mobility
3. Culture, history, and heritage
4. Family, parenting, and youth crime
5. Media, advertising, and public information
6. Economy, international business, and technology
7. Government policy, regulation, and public spending
8. Environment and resource use

For collocation generation, the best next step is not to create one broad "2026" pack. A more useful structure is to create focused sub-topic packs that map directly to essay arguments: causes, consequences, policy solutions, advantages/disadvantages, and discuss-both-views comparisons.

## Recommended Collocation Packs

### 1. Education Pathways and Literacy

Use for: Mar 14, May 16, Mar 28, Mar 7.

Argument needs:
- compare university education with immediate employment
- discuss national priorities in public education
- explain why young people move for better study opportunities
- link parenting education to youth outcomes

Core lexical fields:
- higher education, tertiary education, vocational training
- adult literacy, basic literacy skills, functional illiteracy
- employment prospects, labour-market needs, practical experience
- unequal access to educational resources, rural schools, social mobility
- public funding, educational priorities, long-term national development

### 2. Parenting, Child Development, and Youth Crime

Use for: Jan 31, Mar 7.

Argument needs:
- explain why permissive parenting harms children
- connect family education with crime prevention
- discuss limits of parenting-only solutions

Core lexical fields:
- permissive parenting, overindulgent parents, lack of boundaries
- parental supervision, emotional resilience, self-discipline
- moral judgement, respect for rules, delayed gratification
- juvenile delinquency, antisocial behaviour, peer influence
- rehabilitation, early intervention, community support

### 3. Urban Mobility, Road Safety, and Transport Policy

Use for: Feb 7, Feb 28, Mar 28, Apr 25, May 23.

Argument needs:
- evaluate free public transport as a congestion solution
- explain road law violations and propose measures
- discuss migration from rural areas to cities
- weigh transport against technology in global business
- discuss environmental limits on air travel

Core lexical fields:
- traffic congestion, commuting patterns, public transport networks
- road safety laws, traffic violations, speeding, distracted driving
- law enforcement, speed cameras, stricter penalties
- rural-urban migration, urban infrastructure, regional inequality
- logistics networks, supply chains, air travel, carbon emissions

### 4. Culture, History, Heritage, and Architecture

Use for: Jan 24, Mar 21, Apr 11.

Argument needs:
- balance modern urban development with cultural identity
- discuss public access to historical sites
- defend the value of historical research

Core lexical fields:
- cultural identity, traditional architectural styles, urban character
- historic districts, local heritage, collective memory
- historical sites, public access, visitor numbers, conservation work
- heritage preservation, physical deterioration, restoration costs
- historical research, lessons from the past, informed decision-making

### 5. Media, Advertising, and Information Literacy

Use for: Jan 10, Apr 18.

Argument needs:
- explain why advertising is less persuasive
- evaluate whether this is positive or negative
- argue that news remains relevant to daily life

Core lexical fields:
- advertising influence, consumer awareness, emotional appeal
- online reviews, product information, informed choices
- media literacy, skepticism toward advertisements, brand trust
- news coverage, current affairs, public awareness
- critical thinking, civic participation, practical decision-making

### 6. Economy, Global Business, and Technology

Use for: Mar 14, May 23, Apr 25.

Argument needs:
- compare academic preparation with workforce participation
- compare transport infrastructure with digital technology
- explain why air travel supports global economic activity

Core lexical fields:
- international business, global trade, cross-border cooperation
- cheap and fast transportation, logistics efficiency, movement of goods
- digital communication, advanced technology, remote collaboration
- productivity, competitiveness, market access
- tourism, business travel, international supply chains

### 7. Government Policy, Regulation, and Public Spending

Use as a cross-topic pack for most questions.

Argument needs:
- discuss whether governments should insist, restrict, fund, enforce, or regulate
- produce balanced alternatives to extreme policies

Core lexical fields:
- allocate public funds, impose restrictions, enforce regulations
- policy intervention, targeted measures, balanced approach
- financial burden on taxpayers, operating costs, public services
- stricter penalties, public awareness campaigns, regulatory framework
- evidence-based policy, long-term social benefits

### 8. Environment, Resources, and Sustainable Travel

Use for: Apr 25, Feb 7, May 23.

Argument needs:
- discuss pollution and resource consumption from air travel
- link public transport to lower emissions
- evaluate whether restriction or innovation is better

Core lexical fields:
- carbon emissions, fossil fuels, resource consumption
- environmental impact, carbon footprint, sustainable aviation
- technological innovation, fuel-efficient aircraft, cleaner energy
- environmental regulations, offset schemes, greener transport
- economic benefits vs environmental costs

## Priority For Next Collocation Generation

Recommended order:

1. Culture/history/heritage pack, because the current topic bank is likely to need more specific architecture and historical-site language.
2. Transport/road safety/urban mobility pack, because several 2026 questions depend on precise transport policy vocabulary.
3. Education pathways/adult literacy pack, because education is frequent and needs sub-topic precision beyond generic school language.
4. Parenting/youth crime pack, because it bridges Society, Education, and Government.
5. Media/advertising/news pack, because it supports both consumer and civic-information essays.
6. International business/logistics/technology pack, because it links Economy and Technology.
7. Environment/aviation pack, because it is narrower but high-value for transport-environment essays.
8. Government policy language pack, because it can be reused across all topics as argument glue.

## Suggested Card Design For Each Pack

Each sub-topic pack should include:

- 20-30 core noun phrases
- 15-20 adjective + noun collocations
- 15-20 verb + noun collocations
- 10 argument sentence frames
- 5 contrast frames for discuss-both-views essays
- 5 policy solution frames
- 5 cause/consequence frames

Suggested data tags:

- `source`: `2026-Jan-May-Task2`
- `topicCluster`: one of the eight clusters above
- `questionIds`: date-based IDs such as `2026-01-10`
- `taskType`: `agree-disagree`, `discuss-both-views`, `reasons-measures`, `positive-negative`, `advantages-disadvantages`

## Coverage Gap Compared With Current App Topics

The current app's 10-topic system can absorb all these questions, but several sub-topics need sharper coverage:

- Arts needs more heritage, historical-site, architecture, and cultural-identity language.
- Urbanization needs more traffic congestion, road safety, public transport, and rural-urban migration language.
- Education needs more university-vs-work, adult literacy, and unequal-resource language.
- Society needs more parenting, boundaries, youth development, and juvenile-crime language.
- Media needs more advertising skepticism, news relevance, and information literacy language.
- Economy needs more logistics, international business, supply chains, and technology-enabled commerce language.
- Environment needs more aviation, fuel use, carbon emissions, and sustainable travel language.
